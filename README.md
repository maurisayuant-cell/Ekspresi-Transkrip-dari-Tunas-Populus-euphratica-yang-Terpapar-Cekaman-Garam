# Differential Expression Analysis of Populus euphratica Under Salt Stress

## Background
Populus euphratica is a salt-tolerant tree species that naturally grows in saline environments. Understanding its transcriptomic response to salinity stress is important to uncover molecular mechanisms involved in osmotic regulation, ion transport, and cellular defense.

## Objective
To identify differentially expressed genes (DEGs) between salt-treated and control samples using the GEO2R web-based platform.

## Dataset Information
- GEO Accession: GSE52305
- Organism: Populus euphratica
- Data Type: Transcriptomics (Microarray)
- Platform: GPL4359

## Experimental Design
Comparison:
- Group 1: Control samples
- Group 2: Salt-treated samples 

## DEG Analysis Parameters
Analysis was performed using GEO2R with the following criteria:
- Multiple testing correction: Benjamini–Hochberg (FDR)
- Adjusted p-value (adj. p) < 0.05
- |log2 Fold Change| > 1

Reproducibility:
The analysis was repeated three times using consistent grouping and parameter settings to ensure reproducibility of DEG trends.

## Results Summary
- Total significant DEGs: 80.008 genes
- Up-regulated genes: 28.575 genes
- Down-regulated genes: 32.821 genes

## Biological Interpretation
Up-regulated genes were mainly associated with osmotic stress response, ion transport regulation, and cellular protective mechanisms. Down-regulated genes may reflect suppression of growth-related pathways under stress conditions.

These results suggest that salt stress triggers transcriptomic reprogramming in Populus euphratica to enhance stress adaptation and maintain cellular homeostasis.
